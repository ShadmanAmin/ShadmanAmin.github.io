I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Geography at UC Santa Barbara, working under Kelly Caylor and Anna Trugman. My dissertation asks a very simple question: how do trees survive a drought? Answering it has meant hauling LiDAR, thermal cameras, sapflow probes and a small solar-powered telemetry network out to the Dangermond Preserve — and then persuading all of it to keep reporting while the local wildlife chews through the cabling.
