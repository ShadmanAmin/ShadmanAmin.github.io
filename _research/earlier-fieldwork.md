Before ecohydrology there were two projects that, on paper, look like a
wandering CV. In practice they taught me most of what I know about deploying
sensors in places that were not designed for them.

## Urban bats in Copenhagen

At DIS Copenhagen and Københavns Universitet, working with Inger Kærgaard, I
ran field research across six sites of varying anthropogenic disturbance —
including the Round Tower — deploying ultrasonic bat detectors and analysing
the recordings in Anabat Insight to study urban feeding and migration
behaviour. Bats are excellent teachers of the principle that your sampling
design is only as good as your assumptions about when the animal shows up.

## Green frogs on the Allen Islands

With Dr. Catherine Bevier at Colby, I spent a summer in Central
Maine, including the Allen Islands studying the sub-lethal effects of *Batrachochytrium dendrobatidis*
infection on the skin microbiome, morphology and behaviour of the northern
green frog (*Lithobates clamitans*). The work ran from field capture through
partial protein purification, bacterial isolation, cell-free supernatant and
agar challenge assays, and fungal growth analysis — a full trip from the pond
to the bench and back.
