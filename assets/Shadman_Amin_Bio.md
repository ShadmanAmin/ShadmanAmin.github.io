## Summary

Shadman Amin is a PhD candidate in the Department of Geography at UC Santa Barbara, working in the Caylor (WAVEs) Lab and co-advised by Dr. Kelly Caylor and Dr. Anna Trugman. His dissertation research investigates the drought resilience of coast live oak (*Quercus agrifolia*) woodlands at the Jack and Laura Dangermond Preserve (JLDP), integrating multi-temporal LiDAR, UAV-based thermal and multispectral imagery, sapflow sensors, pressure-chamber plant water potential measurements, and a custom-built LoRa/Starlink field telemetry observatory. His work sits at the intersection of ecohydrology, remote sensing, and applied engineering — designing and deploying the physical sensor networks that generate the data he then analyzes computationally on university high-performance computing (HPC) resources.

Beyond ecohydrology, Shadman has broad technical interests spanning thermodynamics, statistical mechanics, computer vision, embedded systems, and general environmental physics, and holds an FAA Part 107 UAS pilot certification.

## Research

### Dissertation: Coast Live Oak Woodland Drought Resilience at Dangermond Preserve

As Project Lead for Dangermond Ecohydrology in the Earth Research Institute's WAVEs Lab (Jan 2024–Present), Shadman designed and deployed an intensive remote ecohydrological observatory at the Jack and Laura Dangermond Preserve at Point Conception, integrating:

- **Multi-temporal LiDAR** and **UAV thermal/multispectral imagery** (monthly DJI Matrice 600 Pro flights across a north-facing to south-facing slope transect) to study individual canopy water use
- **Sapflow sensors** (custom UCSB.sapflow v1.5 hardware, a Beslity et al. 2022 Js⁵ derivative) and **pressure-chamber** plant water potential measurements
- An autonomous **LoRa/Starlink telemetry network** of meteorological stations, soil moisture sensors, and sapflow sensors deployed across the field transect, streaming real-time data to lab servers
- A custom **modular UAS datalogger** for atmospheric boundary layer profiling — measuring wind speed/direction, shortwave/longwave radiation, thermal and multispectral imagery, temperature, and relative humidity — including firmware development and resolution of variable gain offsets and voltage ripple issues on the temperature/RH breakout board
- Full-stack development (backend + frontend) of a real-time data dashboard and an [interactive digital-twin web map](https://dangermond-map.onrender.com/) of the field transect showing live individual tree status

Fieldwork has required significant resilience: re-outfitting the site every few months against wildlife damage to protective equipment caging, and hiking substantial field equipment into the site when storm damage makes roads impassable.

Computationally, Shadman processes large volumes of thermal and multispectral imagery on UCSB's GRIT HPC cluster, and has built and maintained a multi-stage pipeline (`slurm_metforcing_download`) to assemble meteorological forcing data bundles for cross-validating seven evapotranspiration models (PT-JPL, PT-JPL-SM, STIC, MOD16, TSEB, SSEBop, GeeSEBAL), sourced from NEON tower sites. He has become a go-to resource in the department for HPC troubleshooting and workflow support.
